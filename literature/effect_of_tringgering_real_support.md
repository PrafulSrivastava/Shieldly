- **Better option:** an AI assistant that keeps talking **while also triggering real support**
- **Weaker option:** a purely fake overheard AI exchange used only as bluff

# Resources

## Real collective support is the strongest signal in the literature
Phone use and safety tools seem most useful when they support a collective response such as contacting others, sharing location, or coordinating assistance (Just et al., 2019) (Nasar et al., 2007).

## Actual help matters more than safety theater
Safety apps can raise perceived safety without clearly reducing victimization, and at least some app evidence shows improved information but no clear drop in victimization (Capellan et al., 2022). That makes a pure bluff feel closer to theater than protection (Kettrey et al., 2025).

## Clear, explicit help signals work better than vague signals
In bystander research, direct emergency wording attracted more help than vague alternatives (Shotland & Stebbins, 1980). That supports an overheard script that clearly states the problem and location.

## If the follower is motivated by dominance, fake “I am busy on a call” may be too weak
The street harassment literature suggests power and dominance signaling matter (DelGreco et al., 2021) (Nitschke & Lam, 2021). A pursuer may not care that someone is “on a call” unless the call creates accountability or intervention.

## A bluff has a failure mode
If the pursuer keeps following, the victim has less real backup. A system that quietly alerts real people has a better safety margin.

# Conclusion
| Option | Likely deterrence | Safety margin | Evidence strength |
|------|------|------|------|
| AI speaks and quietly summons real support  | Higher | Higher | Moderate by synthesis |
| Fake AI conversation only | Uncertain | Lower | Weak and indirect |

The safest design is probably:

- audible message that signals the situation is recognized
- location stated out loud
- imminent arrival at a staffed or populated place
- simultaneous real outreach to trusted contacts or nearby support
- no taunting, no bluff that depends on the follower believing something unverifiable
